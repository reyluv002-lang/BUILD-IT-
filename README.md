# NEXUS

> **The Network That Cannot Be Shut Down**

## Overview

NEXUS is a decentralized emergency communication platform built for the Build.IT Hackathon. It enables people to communicate even when traditional communication infrastructure—such as cellular networks, internet services, satellites, and routers—is unavailable.

Designed for disaster scenarios and large-scale communication failures, NEXUS creates a peer-to-peer network where nearby devices securely exchange messages without relying on centralized servers.

---

## Problem

During natural disasters, electromagnetic pulses (EMPs), cyberattacks, or infrastructure failures, conventional communication systems become unreliable or completely unavailable. This leaves communities unable to coordinate rescue efforts, share critical information, or contact loved ones when communication is needed the most.

---

## Solution

NEXUS establishes a resilient offline communication network by allowing devices to connect directly with one another. Each connected device acts as a communication node, forwarding messages across the network until they reach their destination. As more devices join, the network becomes stronger, extending communication without requiring any existing infrastructure.
